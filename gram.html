Gram

Gram, commonly known as chickpea (Cicer arietinum), is an important legume crop widely consumed for its high nutritional value. It is a rich source of protein, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making it a staple food in many countries.

Gram is available in various forms such as whole chickpeas, split chickpeas (chana dal), and chickpea flour (besan). It supports digestive health, helps control blood sugar, and improves heart health.

Why Gram Is Grown in This Season

Gram is mainly grown during the Rabi season as it requires cool and dry climatic conditions. It has low water requirements and adapts well to semi-arid regions.

Benefits of Growing Gram

Gram improves soil fertility by fixing nitrogen in the soil. It has good market demand and provides stable income to farmers.

Gram Farming Tips

  • Select certified disease-resistant seeds.
  • Use well-drained loamy soil.
  • Avoid excess irrigation.
  • Apply organic manure before sowing.
  • Harvest when pods are fully mature.
