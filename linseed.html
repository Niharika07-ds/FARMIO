Linseed

Linseed, also known as flaxseed (Linum usitatissimum), is a valuable oilseed crop cultivated for its seeds and fiber. It is highly nutritious and widely used for health and industrial purposes.

Linseed is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, lignans, and dietary fiber. It supports heart health, reduces inflammation, and helps lower cholesterol levels.

Why Linseed Is Grown in This Season

Linseed is mainly grown during the Rabi season as it prefers cool and dry climates. Excess moisture and high humidity can affect seed quality.

Benefits of Growing Linseed

Linseed has good market demand for oil extraction and medicinal use. It requires low water input and provides good returns to farmers.

Linseed Farming Tips

  • Select certified, high-yield seed varieties.
  • Grow in well-drained loamy soil.
  • Avoid waterlogging.
  • Apply organic manure before sowing.
  • Harvest when capsules turn brown.
