Groundnut

Groundnut, also known as peanut, is an important oilseed and legume crop grown widely in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family and is rich in protein, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals. Groundnuts are unique because their pods develop underground.

Why Groundnut Is Grown in This Season

Groundnut grows best in warm climates with moderate rainfall. It requires temperatures between 20°C and 30°C for proper growth and pod formation. Excess moisture or cold conditions can affect yield, so it is mainly grown in summer or monsoon seasons.

Benefits of Growing Groundnut

Groundnut improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation and provides good income to farmers. It has high market demand for oil extraction, snacks, and food products.

Groundnut Farming Tips

  • Use certified, high-yielding seeds.
  • Grow in sandy or loamy, well-drained soil.
  • Ensure proper spacing for pod development.
  • Provide moderate irrigation; avoid waterlogging.
  • Apply organic manure for better yield.
  • Control pests and diseases regularly.
