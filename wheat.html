Wheat

Wheat is a widely cultivated cereal crop and a staple food for much of the world’s population. It belongs to the genus Triticum and was first cultivated in the Fertile Crescent around 9600 BC. Wheat grains are rich in carbohydrates and provide energy for daily activities.

Wheat is also a major source of plant protein and dietary fiber when consumed as whole grain. It contains essential nutrients such as iron, magnesium, and B-complex vitamins.

Why Wheat Is Grown in This Season

Wheat is mainly grown during the winter (Rabi) season. Cool temperatures during growth and warm weather during maturity help in better grain formation and high yield.

Benefits of Growing Wheat

Wheat has high market demand and provides stable income to farmers. It improves food security and can be stored for long periods.

Wheat Farming Tips

  • Select disease-resistant seed varieties.
  • Prepare fertile, well-drained soil.
  • Sow seeds at proper depth and spacing.
  • Provide timely irrigation.
  • Apply fertilizers as recommended.
